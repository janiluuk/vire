# ROADMAP.md
# Sparkki UX & Product Transformation Roadmap
## For Cursor / Windsurf / Claude Code / Codex Agents

---

# PROJECT GOAL

Transform Sparkki from a technical Linux/upcycling service into a calm, trustworthy, visually engaging technology renewal platform for normal people.

The site should feel:
- approachable
- visually guided
- emotionally safe
- understandable without technical knowledge
- modern Nordic
- practical and trustworthy

The user should immediately understand:
- what Sparkki does
- whether their computer is still useful
- how the process works
- what practical benefits they gain

---

# CORE EXPERIENCE PRINCIPLES

## PRIORITIZE

- clarity over technical accuracy
- reassurance over complexity
- outcomes over specifications
- visual learning over long text
- practical benefits over ideology
- confidence over choices

---

# AVOID

- hacker aesthetics
- distro-first messaging
- technical jargon
- repair-shop feeling
- dense text walls
- overwhelming navigation
- excessive feature lists
- cyberpunk/neon UI

---

# TARGET EMOTIONAL FEELING

The website should feel like:
- calm technology
- modern Nordic service
- simple and trustworthy
- “your computer can still be good”
- technology without stress

---

# DESIGN LANGUAGE

## Visual Inspiration

Think:
- Nordic appliance brand
- digital wellbeing startup
- modern sustainable service
- approachable Apple-like simplicity
- calm Finnish aesthetics

---

## UI Characteristics

- soft rounded corners
- generous spacing
- large typography
- warm neutral colors
- subtle motion
- visual storytelling
- low cognitive load

---

# PHASE 1 — HOMEPAGE RESTRUCTURE

## Goal

The homepage must explain the service in under 10 seconds.

---

## Main Messaging Shift

DO NOT lead with:
- Linux
- open source
- distro names
- technical terminology

LEAD WITH:
- faster old computers
- simpler everyday use
- no forced online accounts
- stability
- Finnish support
- affordable longevity

---

## Required Homepage Structure

### 1. Hero Section

Headline should focus on:
- speed
- simplicity
- usefulness

Example direction:
“Vanha kone voi tuntua taas nopealta.”

Subheadline:
- simple language
- practical outcomes
- reassurance

CTA:
- compatibility check
- service overview

---

### 2. Instant Compatibility Checker

This is one of the highest priority features.

Users should instantly know:
“Can my computer still work smoothly?”

---

## Compatibility Wizard Requirements

### UX

- no account required
- no technical knowledge required
- mobile-first
- card-based flow
- visually guided
- instant result

---

## Suggested Flow

### Step 1
“How old is your computer?”

Options:
- 0–2 years
- 3–5 years
- 6–8 years
- 9+ years

---

### Step 2
“What do you use it for?”

Options:
- internet
- banking
- school
- music
- office
- movies
- lightweight gaming

---

### Step 3
Result card

Examples:
- excellent candidate
- works well with SSD upgrade
- good for basic use
- probably not worth upgrading

---

## Implementation Notes

Build reusable component:
`CompatibilityWizard`

Should support:
- animations
- progress states
- expandable explanations
- recommendation cards
